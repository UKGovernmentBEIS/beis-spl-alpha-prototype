Maternity leave and pay

Maternity Leave is available for up to 52 weeks. Statutory Maternity Pay is available for 39 weeks. You get 90% of your average weekly earnings for the first 6 weeks and £148.68 (or 90% of your average weekly earnings if you earn less than £165.20) for the next 33 weeks.

You must take maternity leave and pay in one continous block. You can start 11 weeks before the expected week of birth. You must take 2 weeks of maternity leave after your baby is born.

Shared parental leave and pay

Shared Parental Leave is available for up to 50 weeks minus any maternity leave taken by the mother. Statutory Shared Parental Pay is available for up to 37 weeks minus any Statutory Maternity Pay taken by mother. You get £148.68 or 90% of your average weekly earnings if you earn less than £165.20.

You can take shared parental leave and pay in blocks with periods of work in between. You and your partner can be off work at the same time, or one after the other.

Paternity leave and pay

Paternity Leave is available for 2 weeks. Statutory Paternity Pay is available for up to 2 weeks. You get £148.68 or 90% of your average weekly earnings if you earn less than £165.20.

You must take paternity leave in the first 8 weeks after the baby is born.

Plan shared parental leave and pay

Use this calendar to plan shared parental leave and pay alongside maternity and paternity leave and pay. The calendar is based on your child's birth week.

Click the cells to select the weeks each parent wants to take off work, starting with the mother's maternity leave.

If you're taking more than 37 weeks of leave, you can also select which leave weeks are paid and unpaid. The statutory pay amount is indicative only.

How shared parental leave and pay works

Shared parental leave and pay is created from unused maternity leave and pay.

How much you can get

Parents can split up to 50 weeks of shared parental leave and 37 weeks of statutory shared parental pay between them.

Statutory shared parental pay is £148.68 per week, or 90% of your average weekly earnings, whichever is lower.

When you can start

You can start shared parental leave any time after the child's birth, as long as the mother has ended her maternity leave (either by returning to work or committing to an end date in writing).

This means the partner can start shared parental leave while the mother is on maternity leave.

How you can take it

Both the mother and the partner can take shared parental leave in blocks, either at the same time or one after the other.
